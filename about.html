How We Work

The Five Pillars

  1. 01

    Preservation

    The primary goal. Every system we apply is chosen for its ability to help reduce oxidation, UV damage, and salt degradation over time. We treat the vessel's surface as infrastructure, not cosmetic detail.

  2. 02

    Performance

    A protected surface performs differently. Hydrophobic coatings shed salt and marine contamination more efficiently, reducing the frequency and intensity of maintenance required between service intervals.

  3. 03

    Precision

    No blanket solutions. Each engagement begins with a physical inspection of the vessel's actual condition. The correct protection system is matched to the surface — not sold for its price point.

  4. 04

    Luxury Care

    We operate in environments where vessels represent significant financial and personal investment. Our processes, communication, and standards reflect that context throughout every engagement.

  5. 05

    Longevity

    Protection that extends the working life of gelcoat and topcoat. Exact lifespans communicated clearly — 12–24 months for ceramic, 36–60 months for PPF. No rounding up, no marketing inflation.

Built for Here

Egypt's Marine Conditions

Egypt's Red Sea and Mediterranean coastlines create a uniquely demanding environment for marine surfaces. Year-round UV radiation is among the highest in the region. Salt concentration is elevated. Ambient temperatures accelerate the degradation of unprotected gelcoat, topcoat, and teak.

The vessels operating in these conditions require systems engineered for them — not automotive-derived products adapted for occasional water exposure. Our protection systems are selected and applied specifically for saltwater environments, high-UV intensity, and the maintenance realities of marina-based ownership.

The result is protection that lasts as specified, in conditions that would shorten the effective life of conventional approaches.
